Should I pay off my Visa or my car loan first?
Great question. In most cases, you will want to focus on the debt with the highest interest rate first. That is typically a credit card. If your Visa rate is 19.99% and your car loan is 6.5%, paying down the Visa saves you more in interest. This is called the avalanche method. However, if the car loan balance is very small, paying it off first can free up monthly cash flow. Would you like me to walk you through both scenarios with your actual numbers?

Our Story

Why we built this

Financial stress is the number one source of stress for Canadians. It affects sleep, relationships, health, and every decision a person makes. And at the root of most financial stress is credit. Misunderstood, mismanaged, or never explained.

Credit Bright was built to close that gap. Not with another course that delivers information you could find online for free. With a credential. Earned through assessment, grounded in behavioral science, and built entirely on Canadian credit law and guidelines.

We believe the knowledge changes how you think. The tools help you act. And the certificate proves it. To yourself, to your employer, to your mortgage broker, to anyone who needs to know you understand how credit works in this country.

Substance over flash

Every design choice, every interaction, every word exists because it helps you learn. Nothing is there to look impressive. Everything is there to be useful.

Canadian to the core

Built on Canadian credit bureau rules, FCAC guidelines, and provincial consumer protection law. We do not adapt American content. We build from Canadian ground up.

Earned, not given

Our certificates require passing an assessment. That is what makes them worth something. A credential that anyone can get is a credential nobody respects.

Completely independent

We do not sell credit products. We do not earn affiliate commissions. We do not run ads. Our only interest is that you understand how credit works. That independence is what makes the education trustworthy.
